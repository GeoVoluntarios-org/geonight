Terra Incognita Game-Contest

Children do not play to learn, they learn because they play.

Between March 31 and April 1, 20022, group game sessions (up to 10 people) will be organized for one hour from 5:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m. (CET).

Collectively each group will have to reveal the identity and location of a series of places located in different points of the world. To discover them, geographic content clues will be given (characteristics of the physical environment and the human environment, potentialities, risks, environmental problems, etc.).

The winning groups will receive a set of gifts linked to Geography as a prize.

    About this initiative

    Geography is the exciting science that moves the world and anecdotally quite unknown by most people. If you stop anyone on the street and ask: "Do you know what a person with a degree in Geography can do?", there will be few who can say anything more than: read maps, name capitals, rivers or similar things .

    Professionals in Geography are trained for much more, such as: knowing how to manage the territory , combat climate change and its consequences, fight against natural and technological risks, design geomarketing strategies , use state-of-the-art remote sensing tools, find patterns and relationships between human and natural problems through spatial analysis , making them perfect allies to help achieve the Sustainable Development Goals , and much more.

    With the aim of highlighting geography and professionals in this field, the "Geography Night" ( geonight.net ) is an international initiative in which one evening or night a year events are organized simultaneously around the world .

    In this way, it is intended that anyone has the opportunity to become familiar with the approach of the geographic discipline, its research work and its usefulness as an applied science to solve the great challenges facing the planet .

    Specifically, this page you are visiting is a recreational initiative launched by the Spanish Association of Geography in collaboration with other entities to disseminate the contents of Geography, as well as to promote the connection between the group of geographers and Geography enthusiasts from different parts of the world.

    The game-contest Terra Incógnita has been created by the AGE and the TransUMAntes Network of the UMA, and developed by the Spanish GeoDevelopers community.

    Spanish Association of Geography

    The Spanish Association of Geography is a group of geography professionals whose main purpose is to promote and develop Spanish geographic science and its applications and to disseminate and publicize geographic knowledge in society. Since it was founded in 1975, the AGE has focused its activities on promoting scientific meetings, coordinating the actions of Spanish geography in society and collaborating with other national and international geographic associations. Currently the AGE brings together some 1,090 people linked to geography through teaching, research and the free exercise of the profession of geographer. Among the activities of the AGE is the celebration every two years of the General Congresses, which are hosted by the Departments of Geography of the Spanish universities. In alternate years, the Association organizes conferences to disseminate geography, in order to bring it closer to society and make known its proposals on current issues. The members of the AGE receive complete information about the celebration and results of all the activities and obtain economic advantages in the participation.

    This initiative is organized through its GeoVolunteer Program.

    Bases del concurso

    Participantes:

    El concurso está abierto a jugadores/as jóvenes y adultos/as, de cualquier nivel de formación. El juego está expresado en español.

    Inscripción y fechas a recordar:

    Inscripción: La inscripción es gratuita .

    El plazo de inscripción estará abierto hasta el día 30 de marzo.

    Para jugar y concursar, los jugadores/as deben acceder a la hoja de inscripción (aquí), seleccionar el día y hora de juego que prefieren, e inscribirse en la sesión de juego correspondiente.

    Las sesiones se juegan de forma cooperativa en una reunión on-line y cada grupo obtiene una única puntuación conjunta con la que compite con la obtenida por los grupos de jugadores/as de otras sesiones. Cada sesión de juego admite un máximo de 10 jugadores/as

    Las sesiones de juego se celebrarán en formato on-line en distintos tramos horarios de los días 31 de Marzo y 1 de abril de 2022. Los participantes inscritos recibirán una invitación a la sesión on-line en la que se desarrollará el juego en grupo.

    La resolución de los enigmas del juego y los ganadores del concurso se harán públicos en una sesión conjunta con todos los jugadores el día viernes 1 de Abril a las 22 h una vez finalizadas todas las sesiones de juego y coincidiendo con la celebración de Geo-Night en todo el mundo. La invitación a la Sesión Final del Juego-Concurso se adjuntará en el formulario de inscripción en el día de celebración.

    Premios:

    Los grupos ganadores recibirán como premio un conjunto de obsequios vinculados a la Geografía:

    • Libros y Atlas de Geo-Planeta: libros relacionados con viajes, descubrimientos, rutas y otras temáticas geográficas, y atlas de enfoque singular
    • Libros de Lunwerg–Planeta relacionados con propuestas de gamificación de contenidos geográfico (Escape-Maps)
    • Obsequio de la librería especializada en temática geográfica Mapas y Compañía.
